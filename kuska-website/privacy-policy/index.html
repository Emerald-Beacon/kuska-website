Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 14, 2025

At Kuska Autism Services, your family’s privacy, trust, and confidentiality are our top priorities. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information, including how we comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

1. Information We Collect

We may collect personal and health-related information that you voluntarily provide, including:

  • Your name, phone number, email address, and city
  • Information related to your child’s autism diagnosis or care needs
  • Insurance or provider details (if applicable)
  • Any information submitted through contact forms, phone calls, or emails

We may also collect technical data (e.g., IP address, browser type) to improve our website functionality.

2. HIPAA Compliance and Protected Health Information (PHI)

Certain information we collect may qualify as Protected Health Information (PHI) under HIPAA. PHI includes any health-related information that identifies you or your child and relates to diagnosis, treatment, or payment for healthcare services.

Kuska Autism Services complies with all HIPAA regulations. We:

  • Safeguard PHI using administrative, physical, and technical safeguards
  • Limit access to PHI to authorized personnel only
  • Require written authorization before using or disclosing PHI for purposes beyond treatment, payment, or healthcare operations (unless required by law)

3. How We Use Your Information

We use personal and health information solely to:

  • Respond to inquiries and coordinate services
  • Provide individualized support and care
  • Contact you about appointments or service updates
  • Fulfill legal or billing requirements

We do not sell, rent, or share your information for commercial marketing purposes.

4. Sharing and Disclosure of Information

We may share information:

  • With authorized team members or healthcare providers involved in your child’s care
  • With insurance companies or billing entities (with appropriate safeguards)
  • As required by law or in response to a legal request (e.g., court order or subpoena)

All sharing is done in compliance with HIPAA and only on a need-to-know basis.

5. Your Rights Under HIPAA

As a parent or legal guardian, you have the right to:

  • Access and receive copies of your child’s records
  • Request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Request restrictions on how we use or share PHI
  • Receive an accounting of disclosures
  • File a complaint if you believe your privacy rights have been violated

To make a HIPAA-related request, please contact us at 801-980-7970.

6. Security Measures

We implement industry-standard practices and technologies to secure your data. These include encrypted communications, secure data storage, and access controls. Staff are trained on confidentiality and privacy best practices.

7. Children’s Privacy

We only collect information about children with parental or guardian consent. All information is handled in accordance with both general privacy principles and HIPAA requirements.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy as our practices or legal obligations change. We encourage you to review it regularly. The updated version will include a new “Effective Date” at the top.

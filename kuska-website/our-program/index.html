Our Programs

At Kuska Autism Services, we provide high-quality ABA therapy in Utah, serving families in Bountiful, Draper, and surrounding areas. Our programs are rooted in evidence-based Applied Behavior Analysis principles, designed specifically for children with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) or developmental delays. Whether your child needs intensive skill-building or targeted support, we create individualized plans that fit your family's lifestyle, schedule, and goals. With flexible delivery options—including in-home ABA therapy in Utah, clinic-based sessions at either our Bountiful or Draper locations, or a hybrid model—we make effective therapy accessible without long waitlists. Morning and daytime hours are available, so families can start seeing meaningful progress sooner.

Our Services

Comprehensive ABA Programs

Our comprehensive ABA program in Utah follows an intensive model, typically involving 30–35 hours of therapy per week. This approach is ideal for children who benefit from broad, consistent support across multiple developmental domains. A Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) conducts a thorough assessment to identify strengths, challenges, and priority areas. From there, we develop a customized treatment plan with clear, measurable goals. Direct therapy is delivered by trained Registered Behavior Technicians (RBTs) under close BCBA supervision, ensuring high-quality implementation and frequent data review for adjustments. Parents and caregivers receive regular training to generalize skills at home, school, and in the community. Many families in Utah choose this model for younger children or those needing foundational skills in communication, social interaction, and independence—leading to lasting improvements in daily functioning and quality of life.

Focused ABA Programs

For children with more targeted needs, our focused ABA therapy in Utah provides 15–25 hours per week, based on clinical recommendations from the initial assessment and your family's availability. This model concentrates on specific priorities—such as reducing challenging behaviors, improving a key social skill, or building independence in daily routines—while still delivering evidence-based intervention. Sessions are flexible and can be scheduled around school or other commitments. BCBA oversight ensures progress tracking and parent coaching, so skills transfer beyond therapy hours. This approach works well for older children, those with milder support needs, or families transitioning from more intensive programs.
